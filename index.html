How to Survive Group Projects

  • The Procrastinator: "We had a project due tonight? Don't worry guys, let's start now."
  • The Ghost: *They were assigned to your group yet you never knew*
  • The Perfectionist: The person who color-coded the entire project and wrote 10 drafts, you know who I'm talking about.
  • The Overachiever: "Let's present using 3D holograms. Normal slides are just toooo basic."
  • The Free Rider: "I brought snacks… that counts as contributing, right you guys?"

Navigating the Hallway Traffic Jam

  • The Slow Walker: The kid who's walking like our passing periods are just hours long.
  • The Locker Crowd: The kid who's in front of his locker, your locker, just mindlessly scrolling their phone for 15 minutes.
  • The Backpack Swinger: "Sorry for hitting you, my backpack has a mind of its own."
  • The PDA Couple: No comment required.

Decoding Teacher Lingo

  • "I’ll grade this over the weekend": "Expect your grades in about... 3 weeks."
  • "This will be on the test": "Maybe it will, maybe it won’t... good luck."
  • "This is easy": "I have a PhD in this, but you’ll be fine."
  • "This is a group project": You better have some good partners, or you're utterly cooked.

How to Avoid Awkward Small Talk in Class

  • The Weather Update: "The weather is soooo nice today, right?"
  • The Default Homework Question: "Did you do the homework?"
  • The Forced Compliment: "Nice, uh pencil case. Very stylish and demure."
  • The Silent Nod: When you don’t know what to say, so you just nod.. awkwardly.

The Anatomy of a School Lunch

  • The Mystery Meat: "It's meat? At least, we think it is. I'm gonna eat it anyways."
  • The Pizza: Cardboard with a side of cheese and mystery sauce.
  • The Veggie Option: "Soggy broccoli..."
  • The Snack Hoarder: We all know that kid who has a stash of chips in their bag because lunch just isn't it.

How to Handle Crush Moments

  • The Accidental Stare: *Just casually looking* --> Oh no, they saw me!"
  • The Failed Compliment: "Your hair looks... like a mushroom! --> Wait, that is not what I meant."
  • The Classic Trip: When you're walking confidently and them you trip right in front of them. Smooth.
  • The Confused Wave: "Were they waving at me? Nope. Just waving at someone behind me."

Pro Tips: Mastering the Art of High School Drama

Now that you’ve made it through the ups and downs of high school survival, here are some final Pro Tips to keep you ahead of the game:

  • Group Projects: Befriend “The Perfectionist” early. Trust me, they're your ticket to getting things done. Of course nothing is free in life, so maybe give them some incentive via chips or sweets!
  • Hallway Traffic Jams: Try to walk in the corners, next to the lockers. And if you meet the slow walkers, don't be afraid to give them a push! (this is a joke obviously)
  • Decoding Teacher Lingo: When your teacher says, “This will be on the test” = Just study everything..
  • Awkward Small Talk: If all else fails, just smile, nod, and ask about homework. :)
  • School Lunch: Always have backup snacks. If the pizza looks questionable, pull out your own stuff. At least you won't question if the meat is real or not.
  • Crush Moments: We all know the feeling of having a crush. What's the way to deal with them? Don't have one, problem solved.

High school can be a little crazy, but with these tips, you'll be just fine. Remember, highschool is NOT supposed to be perfect. Live and Learn. But most importantly, HAVE FUN!!
