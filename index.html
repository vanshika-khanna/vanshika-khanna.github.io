An Insider's Guide to NYFW

How to Survive Group Projects

  • The Procrastinator: "Oh, we have a project due tomorrow? Let’s start now!"
  • The Ghost: "They were assigned to your group... you haven’t seen them since."
  • The Perfectionist: "So I color-coded the entire project and wrote 10 drafts..."
  • The Overachiever: "Let's present using 3D holograms—powerpoints are too basic."
  • The Free Rider: "I brought snacks… that counts as contributing, right?"

Navigating the Hallway Traffic Jam

  • The Slow Walker: "Just taking my time, while you miss your next class..."
  • The Locker Crowd: "Oh, you needed to get through? My bad, I just needed to check my phone for 15 minutes."
  • The Backpack Swinger: "Sorry for hitting you... my backpack has a mind of its own."
  • The PDA Couple: "Because making out in front of your locker is totally the right place."

Decoding Teacher Lingo

  • "I’ll grade this over the weekend": "Expect your grades in about... 3 weeks."
  • "This will be on the test": "Maybe it will, maybe it won’t... good luck."
  • "This is easy": "I have a PhD in this, but you’ll be fine, right?"
  • "This is a group project": "A test of patience and a lesson in teamwork. You’re on your own."

How to Avoid Awkward Small Talk in Class

  • The Weather Update: "So... it’s sunny outside today, huh?"
  • The Default Homework Question: "Did you do the homework?" Always the last-minute panic lifeline.
  • The Forced Compliment: "Nice... uh, pencil case. Very stylish."
  • The Silent Nod: When you don’t know what to say, so you just nod... awkwardly.

The Anatomy of a School Lunch

  • The Mystery Meat: "It’s... meat? At least, we think it is."
  • The Pizza: "Cardboard with a side of cheese and mystery sauce."
  • The Veggie Option: "Soggy broccoli... yum!"
  • The Snack Hoarder: "Always has a stash of chips in their bag, because lunch just won’t cut it."

How to Handle Cringe-Worthy Crush Moments

  • The Accidental Stare: "Just casually looking... oh no, they saw me!"
  • The Failed Compliment: "Your... hair looks... like a cloud. Wait, what?"
  • The Classic Trip: "Walking confidently... and then you trip right in front of them. Smooth."
  • The Confused Wave: "Were they waving at me? Nope. Just waving at someone behind me."

NYFW can be both amazingly fun & incredibly overwhelming, especially if you’ve never been. Luckily, I’m here to give you an insider’s guide and make your first show a pleasurable experience. By taking my tips and tricks, and following your gut, you’ll have an unforgettable experience!”

Getting Tickets & Picking the Shows

If you’re lucky or connected you can get an invite, sans the price tag. But I wasn’t so lucky or connected my first 2 years so I’m here to help you out. First, plan out which shows are most important to you and make a schedule and this is a biggie: SET A BUDGET. If you’re worrying about blowing your cash the whole time you won’t have fun. Then check out prices, days, and times and prioritize the designers you want to see most. Lastly, purchase your tickets and get excited!

Dressing for the Shows

Always be true to your own sense of style, if you don’t you’ll be uncomfortable the whole time and it will show. Remember, NYFW is about expressing yourself and taking in what the designers have chosen to express through their new lines. Also it’s important to wear shoes you’ll be comfortable in all day. Obviously you want to look good, but you’ll be on your feet all day long, so be prepared.
